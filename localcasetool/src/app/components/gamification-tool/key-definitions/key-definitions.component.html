Key Definitions
CLIMATE EVENTS
Heatwave: A long period of unusually high temperatures that can affect health, water use, and energy demand.
Drought: A lack of rainfall over time, reducing water availability for people, nature, and agriculture.
Coastal event: Flooding or erosion cased by storms, high waves or rising sea levels along the coast.
Energy blackout: A temporary loss of electricity due to extreme conditions or system failures.
Extreme precipitation: Very heavy rainfall in short time, increasing the risks of floods and damage.
Negative public perception: When communities resist or disagree with adaptation actions, slowing process.
Urban heat island: Cities become hotter than nearby areas due to buildings traffic, and limited green.
Wildfires: Uncontrolled fires in forest or dry landscapes, often triggered by heat and drought.
