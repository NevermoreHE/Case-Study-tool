CS3 - Norrbotten County

Context

The county of Norrbotten is Sweden’s northernmost county and is divided into 14 municipalities.
It experiences a subarctic to continental climate characterized by long cold winters with persistent snow cover and short mild summers.
The most relevant sector is biodiversity and natural heritage. It has the largest area as well as the highest proportion of protected nature in Sweden.
Forestry is an important sector, as the 40% of the surface is covered by productive forest, thanks to the cold climate, in which trees grow slowly and get a dense wood of high quality. Reindeer husbandry is also relevant especially for the Sami population.
Energy is a key sector, with high consumption driven by the cold climate, long transport distances, and energy-intensive industry. Most energy comes from fossil fuels, while hydropower, accounts for nearly 90% of electricity produced.
CS1 - Sitia municipality

Context

The Municipality of Sitia is located in the eastern part of the Crete island (Greece) in the regional department of Lasithi.
It is the most “extreme climate hotspot” in Europe, characterized by high winds and almost 300 sunshine days per year, with few rain events.
Agriculture is the main economic activity, with most of the population engaged in farming, livestock, or fishing. Tree crops cover 53% of agricultural land and are highly vulnerable to extreme events caused by climate change.
Another relevant sector is water. A major challenge is maintaining sufficient, high-quality freshwater, as water scarcity from droughts threatens both agriculture and the population, including tourism.
Biodiversity is also a key vulnerable sector. Sitia’s Geopark is one of the most important environmental areas of the Mediterranean.
CS2 - Trentino Province

Context

The Autonomous Province of Trento is located in the north-east of Italy and it is commonly known as Trentino.
The climate varies from temperate continental to alpine climate characterized by cold snowy winters in the mountains and warm summers in the valleys.
The most relevant sector is energy, particularly hydroelectric production. However, about 65% of consumption relies on imported natural gas and oil, and the ski industry adds high seasonal demand.
Tourism is a great economic sector. It is mainly related to ski and to cooler temperatures in summer. In winter, higher temperatures are leading to shorter period of winter sports.
Another key sector is water, closely linked to agriculture, energy production, and tourism, particularly for snowmaking.
CS4 - Murcia region

Context

The Region of Murcia is an autonomous community of Spain located in the southeast of the Iberian Peninsula, on the coast of the Mediterranean Sea.
The climate is defined as semi-arid subtropical Mediterranean, characterized by high temperatures and scarce and irregular rainfall.  
The most relevant sector is agriculture considering its role in the regional economy. The climatic and territorial conditions allow agriculture to acquire great volume of production and productivity of the crops.
Water is a key resource, considering the arid climate conditions. The lack of water is due to the scarcity of rainfall and the water exploitation of the primary sector.
Another important economic activity is tourism. Climate change and water scarcity are two issues that affect the tourism in this region.
CS5 - Danube Delta

Context

Tulcea County is situated in the South-East of Romania, in Dobrogea region, being bordered by the Danube and Black Sea.
The climate is continental, with low precipitation, high atmospheric humidity, hot summers, cold winters and high temperature amplitudes.
Agriculture is the main economic activity in the county. Tulcea is intensively exposed to drought and desertification processes, contributing to the amplification of the rural livelihoods’ vulnerability.
Energy is a profitable sector in terms of productivity. Dobrogea is considered one of the most promising regions in Europe in terms of wind potential.
Another relevant sector is water management. The region has 115 bodies of surface water.Nevertheless these water bodies are often polluted due to inadequate wastewater systems, intensive agriculture, industrial activities, and poor waste and sewage management.
